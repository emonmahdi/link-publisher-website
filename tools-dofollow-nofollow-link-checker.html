What Is a Dofollow Link?

A dofollow link is a hyperlink that allows search engines to follow it and attribute value to the linked page. This value, often referred to as "link equity" or "link juice," helps improve the linked page's search engine ranking. Essentially, a dofollow link is a digital vote of confidence, signaling to search engines that the linked content is valuable and relevant.

Because dofollow links can significantly boost a website's authority and visibility, acquiring them is a primary goal for many marketers. However, it's important to remember that while dofollow links are influential, they are just one of many factors that search engines consider when determining rankings.

By default, all links are dofollow unless specifically marked otherwise (e.g., with the nofollow attribute).

What is a Nofollow Link?

A nofollow link is a hyperlink that instructs search engines not to pass link equity (ranking power) to the linked page. While originally intended to combat spam, Google now treats nofollow as a hint, meaning it might still consider some value from these links.

Nofollow links are commonly found in comments, ads, sponsored content, and user-generated content. While they don't directly boost SEO like dofollow links, they can still drive traffic and enhance brand visibility.

It's important to note that other link attributes like sponsored and ugc have been introduced to provide more specific information about the link's nature.

While dofollow links remain highly coveted in SEO, many marketers also recognize the value of nofollow links in their overall strategy.

What is the difference between dofollow and nofollow links?

Dofollow links boost SEO rankings - while nofollow links do not pass SEO value but can drive traffic.

How does a nofollow link look?

A nofollow link includes the rel="nofollow" attribute, telling search engines not to follow or pass SEO value.

How does a dofollow link look?

A dofollow link does not have special attributes and allows search engines to follow it.
